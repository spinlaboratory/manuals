## Overview ##
The QLP EPR probe head (QLP-1.6 or QLP-2.5) is an EPR probe head for pulsed Q-Band (34 - 36 GHz) EPR spectroscopy. The probe is particularly useful to perform pulsed dipolar experiments such as [Double Electron-Electron Resonance (PELDOR/DEER)](/probes/QLP/80_references/#peldor) experiments, [Relaxation Induced Dipolar Modulation Enhancement (RIDME)](/probes/QLP/80_references/#ridme) experiments, etc.

Pulsed Electron Paramagnetic Resonance (EPR) spectroscopy using high-power microwave pulses or arbitrary waveform generated (AWG) broadband pulses require a large resonator bandwidth (low resonator Q) to ensure efficient excitation and to avoid distortion of the pulse shape. However, a large resonator bandwidth often comes at the cost of a reduced microwave conversion factor. Loop-Gap Resonators (LGR) combine all these desired features together with an excellent field homogeneity across the sample. This is especially important when using AWG generated broadband pulses.

At the heart of the QLP probe head is a Loop-Gap Resonator (LGR), with a large conversion factor, and a large homogenous B<sub>1e</sub> microwave field across the entire sample volume to improve the performance of pulsed EPR experiments. The resonator is the ideal choice when using pulses generated by an Arbitrary Waveform Generator (AWG).

An overview of the QLP probe is given in the figure shown above. For low-temperature measurements, the probe can be operated inside a cryostat. The QLP probe head is compatible with commonly used cyrostats, such as the [Oxford Instruments](https://www.oxinst.com/) cryostat model CF935 or the integrated VTI of the Compact-Q spectrometer.

The main features of the probe are:
* The loop-gap resonator
* Integrated temperature sensor close to the sample space
* Direct WR-28 waveguide connection
* Iris adjustment. Here the probe is shown with a micrometer screw to adjust the iris position. By default the probe is delivered with remote control of the iris.


### Optical Access

Optical access to the sample location for e.g. light/laser irradiation is possible in several different ways:

1. Through the sample holder/stick (for fiber access only).
2. Free-space access through the bottom of the resonator.
3. Free-space access perpendicular to the sample axis. The probe has a small hole (1.5 mm diameter) for direct light access.


### Temperature Sensor ###

The QLP probe head has a built-in, calibrated [Cernox](https://www.lakeshore.com/products/categories/overview/temperature-products/cryogenic-temperature-sensors/cernox) temperature sensor. The connector for the temperature controller is located at the top of the probe. A cable is provided together with the probe to connect the probe to a temperature controller. 

More information about the pin-out can be found in the section [Temperature Sensor](/probes/temperature_sensor/).
